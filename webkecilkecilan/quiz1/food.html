Local Food

Tahu Takwa

Kediri is widely known as the "City of Tofu," and its most iconic culinary specialty is Tahu Takwa. This yellow-colored tofu has a firm texture on the outside yet remains soft on the inside. It is made from high-quality soybeans and is usually marinated with turmeric, which gives it its distinctive golden color. Tahu Takwa can be enjoyed in many ways: fried until crispy, cooked in soup, or served as a side dish with rice and sambal. Its savory taste and unique texture make it a must-try dish when visiting Kediri.

Nasgor Arang

The rice is usually stir-fried with a mixture of sweet soy sauce, shallots, garlic, eggs, chicken or beef slices, and fresh vegetables. The smoky flavor blends perfectly with the rich seasonings, creating a nostalgic and authentic taste that has been loved for generations.
